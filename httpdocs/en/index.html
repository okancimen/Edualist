Why Edualist?

Doğru okulu seçmek, yalnızca web sitelerini karşılaştırıp broşür okumaktan çok daha fazlasını gerektirir. Çocuğunuzun başarılı ve mutlu olabileceği doğru eğitim kurumunu bulmak, birçok farklı faktörün dikkate alınmasını gerektirir.

En iyi kararı vermek için şu unsurlara yakından bakıyoruz:

  • Öğrenci: Çocuğunuzun akademik profili, dil hazır bulunuşluk seviyesi, öğrenme stili ve kişisel özellikleri.
  • Akademik Program: Çocuğunuz için ideal olan müfredatın seçimi (IB, Amerikan, İngiliz, Fransız vb.) ve uzun vadeli üniversite yönelimi.
  • Aile: Eğitim kurumundan beklentileriniz, okul için ayırdığınız bütçe ve okulun vizyon ve misyonunun sizin ve çocuğunuzun hedefleriyle ne kadar örtüştüğü.

Edualist Avantajı

Choosing the right international school goes far beyond simply comparing websites and reading brochures. Finding the right place for your child to succeed means considering many different factors.

To make the best decision, we look closely at:

  • The Student: Your child’s unique academic profile, language readiness, learning style, and emotional adaptability.
  • The Academics: The exact curriculum fit (IB, American, British, etc.) and long-term university directions.
  • The Family: Your core expectations, budget requirements, and ideal lifestyle or location in your new city.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which countries does Edualist advise on for international school placement?

Edualist provides international school placement and adaptation support for families relocating worldwide, including Dubai & UAE, UK, Canada, Germany, France, the Netherlands, Italy, and Singapore.

How much do international school fees in Dubai cost?

Annual tuition fees at Dubai international schools range from AED 25,000 to over AED 120,000. When uniforms, books, transport, and activity fees are added, the true cost can be 20–30% above the advertised tuition price. Edualist prepares a school shortlist matched to the family's budget.

Is my child's English good enough for an international school?

Conversational English and academic English are very different skill sets. Many students described as 'good at English' struggle in an international school environment during the first term. Edualist assesses the child's EAL (English as an Additional Language) level before the move and plans targeted language coaching if needed.

At what age is transitioning to an international school easiest?

Primary school transitions (ages 6–10) typically produce the fastest language and social adaptation. Secondary and high school transitions (ages 11–17) require extra attention to curriculum differences and university planning, but with the right preparation a successful transition is achievable at any age.

Is the IB Diploma accepted by Turkish universities?

Yes. The IB Diploma is recognised by Turkey's YÖK (Council of Higher Education) and scores are used in the equivalency calculation for university admissions. It is also accepted by over 5,000 universities worldwide, making it the most portable curriculum for families who relocate frequently.

How do I get started with Edualist?

The process begins with a free initial consultation. Özlem Çimen evaluates the child's academic background, language level, and family goals, then prepares a personalised school shortlist and roadmap. To request a session, use the contact form on this page.
